Projects

"Progrify" – Project 5€ Business, 2025

In this group project, my teammate and I developed a fitness tracking app, called Progrify, aimed at enhancing existing market solutions by addressing missing or incomplete features. Our focus was on improving user experience through advanced data visualization and engaging gamification elements. We worked collaboratively over the course of one semester, finishing with a final pitch presentation.

"Formiquest" – Designing Digital Products and Experiences (DPME), 2024

As part of this course, we were challenged to apply our UX/UI design skills by creating an app that addresses a specific market gap. The project included developing a functional prototype, conducting brief user testing, and presenting a final pitch. I chose to design a fitness app inspired by the Duolingo system, aiming to motivate users to train consistently and efficiently. The concept focused on healthy habits, using streaks and gamification to encourage long-term engagement.

Project "Zukunftsnavigator", 2025

In collaboration with the Bavarian Foresight Institute, we participated in the development of an interactive, game-inspired platform that helps SMEs (Small and Medium Enterprise) build future skills – both regionally in Bavaria and beyond. The website combines online and offline elements such as a trend database, templates, videos, and workshop formats. An integrated forum fosters exchange and long-term use of the content. As the UX design team, we created a target group–oriented, gamified, and user-friendly concept, integrating AI assistants, nudging elements, and clear information structures to make the introduction to trend research and foresight simple, engaging, and motivating.

Project "Automated-Shuttlebus", 2023

As part of the Human-Machine Interaction course and the VAMOS study, our team explored user interaction with autonomous shuttle buses. We designed and developed a 3D model of the shuttle in Blender and built an interactive Unity simulation, where test participants could move through the bus in a game-like environment, board and exit, operate a touch display to accept a parcel, and retrieve it from a delivery hatch. The bus displays showed live trip information, and our team designed all interface screens for both passenger and parcel-handling features. The concept combined passenger transport and last-mile delivery, simulating a seamless and engaging autonomous mobility experience.

Project "CloseToReality", 2024

As part of the Close to Reality project and study, our team developed a Unity-based VR simulation of a passenger drone traveling from Ingolstadt to Munich. The goal was to investigate preferred seating configurations, explore user needs and expectations for short-distance passenger drones, and assess overall comfort. Testing was conducted using a VR headset, making the study of motion sickness effects a key focus. Participants experienced the full flight virtually, enabling realistic feedback on seating layout, cabin design, and in-flight experience.

Product Design – Emergency Bracelet Concept, 2023

In the Product Design course, we were tasked with identifying a problem and creating a device to solve it. I designed an emergency wristband in Blender, intended for elderly or disabled individuals. In case of incidents such as a fall, users can press a button to alert emergency services. Two small integrated displays provide real-time healthcare data, such as heart rate and blood pressure, offering both safety and valuable health monitoring in a compact, wearable design.

Product Design – Plant-Assistant Concept, 2023

In the Product Design course, we were challenged to identify a problem and create an innovative device to solve it. I designed a leaf-shaped soil sensor that measures nutrient levels, moisture, and light exposure. Using the connected device, users can scan their plant and receive tailored care information based on the specific plant type and its needs, ensuring optimal growth and health.

Menu poster for "Nest" Event Location Ingolstadt, 2025

For the Nest Event Location, I designed the drinks menus for the Take Off event. This included customizing the layout, content, and pricing to fit the event’s theme. The finished menus were printed and displayed throughout the venue, contributing to a consistent and appealing event atmosphere.

Visualization for "Nest" Event Location Ingolstadt, 2025

I designed a clear, visual guide for staff, detailing how to set up the bar and stock the beverage drawers. This ensures nothing is missing and makes it easy to see exactly what is needed. The guide is especially helpful for new team members and is displayed for everyone as a laminated poster.
